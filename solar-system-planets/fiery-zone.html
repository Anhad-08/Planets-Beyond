MERCURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the sun and has extreme temperatures. During the day, it can get as hot as 430°C (800°F), and at night it can drop to -180°C (-290°F) because it has a very thin atmosphere. It's the smallest planet in our solar system and its surface is covered in craters. Mercury orbits the sun quickly, taking just 88 Earth days to go all the way around. It also has a large iron core that creates a weak magnetic field.

VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is a terrestrial planet and is the closest in mass and size to its orbital neighbour Earth. Venus is notable for having the densest atmosphere of the terrestrial planets, composed mostly of carbon dioxide with a thick, global sulfuric acid cloud cover. At the surface it has a mean temperature of 737K (464& degC; 867°F) and a pressure of 92 times that of Earth's at sea level. These extreme conditions compress carbon dioxide into a supercritical state close to Venus's surface.
